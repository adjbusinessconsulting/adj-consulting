Unlocking Customer Value Through RFM Segmentation

Bisnis ritel online sering gagal membedakan pelanggan loyal dari yang hampir tidak aktif. Proyek ini menggunakan RFM Analysis pada 541,909 data transaksi nyata untuk mengidentifikasi segmen pelanggan bernilai tinggi.

Github Repo
dataset
UCI Online Retail · 541,909 rows
tags
RFMEDAsegmentation
Problem Statement

Perusahaan ritel online menghabiskan anggaran promosi secara merata ke semua pelanggan tanpa memahami siapa yang benar-benar berharga.

What We Did

EDA mendalam lalu menghitung skor RFM per pelanggan, diberi skor 1-5 untuk Recency, Frequency, dan Monetary, dikelompokkan menjadi 4 segmen strategis dalam Power BI dashboard.

4// customer segments
541,909// dataset rows
Power BI// live dashboard

So, What Should the Business Do?
recommendation · action plan
01
Reactivate Lapsing / Inactive Customers
Send a time-limited reactivation message (email or push notification) offering a simple incentive such as free shipping or a small discount (10%).
// why it works → Urgency lowers the barrier to return and encourages customers to re-engage with minimal marketing cost.
02
Build Loyalty (Primary Focus)
Introduce a basic loyalty mechanism — member card or points system — where customers earn points after each purchase. Points can be collected and exchanged for gifts.
// why it works → Creates a reason to return, shifting behavior from one-time purchases to repeat buying.
03
Increase Spending (Primary Focus)
Offer bundle deals (e.g., phone case + charger at a discounted price) or add-on discounts at checkout. Send email whenever promotions are created.
// why it works → Bundling increases basket size without requiring additional customer acquisition.

Retail Inventory
Store Performance, Inventory Management and Profitability

Dari 485,875 item inventaris, hanya 36.47% yang terjual. Proyek ini menganalisis Looker E-commerce dataset — mengungkap kategori terlaris, brand paling menguntungkan, dan produk slow-moving.

Github Repo
dataset
Looker E-commerce · 485,875 rows
tools
Python · Pandas · Matplotlib · Seaborn
dashboard
Tableau
tags
EDAinventoryABC analysis
Problem Statement

Lebih dari 63% modal terikat di stok yang tidak bergerak. Bisnis perlu memahami produk mana yang menghasilkan profit dan distribution center mana yang paling efisien.

What We Did

EDA end-to-end: feature engineering (profit margin, inventory days), analisis per kategori/brand/DC, ABC Classification, Inventory Aging Bucket, dan monthly sales trend. Diekspor ke Tableau.

485K// total items
36.47%// sold rate
$10.5M// realized revenue
$5.5M// realized profit
29.4d// avg sell time
So, What Should the Business Do?
recommendations
01
Increase stock allocation for high-revenue and high-profit categories
Certain categories contribute the highest revenue. Focus inventory investment here — ensuring top-performing products are never out of stock.
02
Review slow-moving products — discount, bundle, or reduce reorder volume
Capital tied up in slow stock could be redeployed to products that actually sell. Consider discounting, bundling, or stopping reorders.
03
Prioritize brands with consistently high revenue and healthy profit margins
Brand-level performance differs substantially. Focus marketing and shelf space on brands that consistently deliver strong margins.
04
Investigate distribution centers with slower average inventory turnover
DCs may have different turnover speeds, signaling operational inefficiencies or regional demand variation.
05
Use monthly trend patterns to improve procurement and inventory planning
Monthly sales trends reveal fluctuations that support stock planning and seasonal forecasting.

Customer Clustering with K-Means Machine Learning

Menggunakan machine learning untuk menemukan segmen pelanggan tersembunyi dalam data transaksi online retail. K-Means berbasis RFM membagi pelanggan menjadi kelompok yang actionable.

Github Repo
dataset
UCI Online Retail · 541,909 rows
tools
Python · Scikit-Learn · Pandas · Matplotlib
method
K-Means · Elbow Method · RFM
tags
K-Meansclusteringscikit-learn
Problem Statement

Segmentasi manual bersifat subjektif. Proyek ini menggunakan K-Means Clustering untuk menemukan pengelompokan pelanggan yang natural dan sepenuhnya berbasis data.

What We Did

Menghitung fitur RFM, normalisasi dengan StandardScaler, menentukan k optimal dengan Elbow Method dan Silhouette Score, lalu menjalankan K-Means dengan k=3.

Predicting Food Delivery Time with ML

Platform delivery sering memberikan estimasi waktu yang tidak akurat. Proyek ini membangun model ML untuk memprediksi waktu pengiriman — mempertimbangkan jarak, cuaca, traffic, dan pengalaman kurir.

Github Repo
dataset
Food Delivery Times · 1,000 orders
tools
Python · Scikit-Learn · Pandas · Matplotlib
best model
Linear Regression · R² = 0.82
tags
regressionML pipelinescikit-learn
Problem Statement

Jika ETA tidak akurat, pelanggan kecewa, restoran dapat keluhan, dan tim operasional kesulitan mengalokasikan kurir. Model prediksi ML memungkinkan ETA yang lebih realistis.

What We Did

Pipeline ML end-to-end: EDA, feature engineering (Is_Rush_Hour, Distance × Prep_Time), preprocessing dengan StandardScaler + OneHotEncoder, membandingkan 3 model regresi.

82%// R² accuracy
±6 min// avg error
3// models compared

So, What Should the Business Do?
business recommendations
01
Use predictive ETA models in the app
Show customers more realistic delivery estimates. The model predicts with R²=0.82 and MAE of only ±6 minutes.
// why it works → Realistic ETAs reduce customer disappointment and build platform trust.
02
Allocate couriers more strategically
Assign experienced couriers to difficult routes or busy periods. Distance and courier experience are both significant factors.
// why it works → Matching the right courier to the right route improves performance.
03
Adjust ETA during peak hours
Incorporate rush hour and traffic conditions into operational planning. ETAs should automatically adjust for these factors.
// why it works → Proactive adjustments reduce customer complaints before they happen.
04
Monitor preparation time together with distance
Delivery delay is not only caused by courier travel but also kitchen readiness. Distance × Prep_Time was one of the strongest predictors.
// why it works → Fixing kitchen bottlenecks can reduce total delivery time just as much as optimizing routes.
05
Use the model as a decision-support tool
The model can help operations teams anticipate delays and improve service quality proactively.
// why it works → Data-driven operations teams respond faster than those relying on intuition alone.
